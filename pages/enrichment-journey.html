Approximately 800,000 people live in the Ecuadorian Rainforest, along the river and tributaries. Access to clean water from the rivers is an important part of their lives.

The Amazon is estimated to have 16,000 tree species and 390 billion individual trees. This Floss Silk/Ceiba/Chorisia tree with it's giant thorns keep Amazonian animals from chewing on the trunks. The thorns also collect dew which then condenses into water where the water runs from the pointy ends, then drops to the soil below, to keep ground roots moist during dry spells.

Bamboo is used for many things by the indigenous people and as a way to harvest rain water which collects in the trunks of the Bamboo and can then be accumulated by cutting a slit in the trunk and let it drain into a container and used for drinking.
